Frequently Asked Questions

How much does window cleaning cost in the Flathead Valley?

Most homes fall between $200 and $500 depending on pane count, stories, screens, and condition. We give a firm price up front — no upcharges on the day of service. Commercial recurring service is typically priced per visit on a flat fee.

How often should I have my windows cleaned?

Most homeowners do twice a year — once in spring after winter grit and once in fall before snow. Lakefront and tree-shaded properties often benefit from quarterly cleaning. Storefronts usually run monthly or every two weeks.

Are you licensed and insured?

Yes. OCS LLC is a licensed Montana business and we carry general liability and workers' comp insurance. We can send a certificate of insurance to property managers and HOAs on request.

Do you do pressure washing too?

Yes — driveways, decks, siding, fences, concrete, and brick. We bundle pressure washing with window cleaning at a discount, since it makes sense to do them in the same visit.

Do you clean windows in winter?

We clean year-round in the Flathead Valley as long as conditions allow — generally above about 25°F and dry. We use winter-safe solutions on cold days and reschedule if it's actively snowing or icing.

What's included in a standard window cleaning?

Interior glass, exterior glass, screen washing, sill wipe-down, and track vacuuming. We use drop cloths inside and walk through the home with you at the end so you can flag anything we missed.

How long does a typical home take?

Most homes take two to four hours depending on pane count and access. Larger lake homes and three-story properties can take a full day. We give a time estimate when we quote so you can plan around it.

Do you offer commercial maintenance contracts?

Yes. Many of our restaurant, office, and storefront customers are on a recurring weekly, monthly, or quarterly schedule. We invoice after each visit or monthly, your choice, and we can work after-hours if needed.

What towns do you serve?

We serve Kalispell, Whitefish, Columbia Falls, Bigfork, and the rest of the Flathead Valley. We're happy to come further out for larger projects — just ask.
