What you get

Professional residential window cleaning is more than spraying glass and walking away. We hand-detail every pane, work the corners and edges where dust collects, vacuum out tracks, and wipe down the sills. The result is glass you can actually see through and frames that don't redeposit grime the next time it rains.

What's included

  • Interior glass cleaned by hand, edge to edge
  • Exterior glass cleaned with a professional squeegee technique
  • Screens removed, hand-washed, and replaced
  • Sills wiped down — wood, metal, or vinyl
  • Tracks vacuumed and detailed
  • Light hard-water spot treatment as needed
  • Drop cloths and shoe covers used inside your home
  • Walk-through with you at the end so you can spot anything we missed

Who it's for

  • Annual or twice-yearly maintenance customers
  • Pre-listing or pre-photo cleans for real estate
  • New homeowners after move-in
  • Anyone tired of streaks from DIY products
  • Vacation homes that need a spring open or fall close

How it works

  1. Step 1 — Free estimate. Call, text, or fill out the form. We'll ask about pane count, stories, screens, and any special concerns. Most quotes go out the same day.
  2. Step 2 — Schedule. We typically book one to two weeks out depending on the season. We'll confirm the day before.
  3. Step 3 — Cleaning day. We arrive on time, lay drop cloths, and work top-to-bottom — exterior first if weather allows, then interior. A typical home takes 2–4 hours.
  4. Step 4 — Walk-through. Before we leave, we walk you through every room. If anything's not perfect, we fix it on the spot.
  5. Step 5 — Pay & schedule next time. Pay by card, check, or transfer. We'll text you a reminder when you're due again.

Common questions

How much does residential window cleaning cost?

Most homes in the Flathead Valley fall between $200 and $500 depending on pane count, story height, screen condition, and whether it's interior, exterior, or both. We give a firm price up front — no upcharges on the day of service.

How often should I have my windows cleaned?

Most homeowners do twice a year — once in spring after winter grit and once in fall before snow flies. Lakefront homes and properties under heavy tree cover often benefit from quarterly cleaning.

Do I need to be home?

Not always. For exterior-only cleans, no. For interior, we just need access. We can also work with garage codes, key safes, or a neighbor.

Will you damage my screens or trim?

We treat finishes carefully and remove screens by hand. If a screen is already torn or a frame is rotted, we'll point it out — we won't make it worse.

What about hard-water stains?

Light staining usually comes out with our standard process. Heavy mineral buildup may need a specialty treatment, which we'll quote separately so you can decide.
