What you get

Customers form an opinion about a business before they step inside, and dirty glass is one of the loudest signals that something's off. We keep storefronts, professional offices, restaurants, retail spaces, and multi-tenant buildings looking sharp on a recurring schedule that fits your hours and your budget.

What's included

  • Exterior storefront glass — hand-detailed
  • Interior office and lobby windows
  • Track and frame cleaning
  • Recurring scheduling: weekly, monthly, or quarterly
  • After-hours or early-morning service available
  • COI (certificate of insurance) on request for property managers
  • Coordination with cleaning crews, security, and tenants
  • Hard-water and post-construction add-ons if needed

Who it's for

  • Storefronts and downtown retail in Kalispell, Whitefish, Bigfork, and Columbia Falls
  • Restaurants and tasting rooms
  • Professional offices — medical, legal, real estate, finance
  • Property managers handling multiple buildings
  • HOAs with shared common-area glass
  • Hospitality — motels, B&Bs, vacation-rental managers

How it works

  1. Step 1 — Walkthrough. We meet on-site, count panes, identify access challenges, and learn your hours and preferences.
  2. Step 2 — Proposal. We send a written proposal with frequency options and a flat per-visit price.
  3. Step 3 — Schedule lock-in. We add you to our route. You don't have to manage anything — we just show up.
  4. Step 4 — Recurring service. We arrive on the agreed cadence. You get clean glass with zero coordination overhead.
  5. Step 5 — Easy billing. Invoiced after each visit or monthly — your choice. Net-15 standard, longer terms negotiable.

Common questions

Do you offer after-hours service?

Yes. Many of our restaurant and retail customers prefer early-morning cleaning before they open. We're happy to schedule that way.

Can you provide a certificate of insurance?

Yes — we'll send a COI to your property manager on request. We carry general liability and workers' comp.

Do you do multi-story buildings?

We handle most two- and three-story commercial buildings with ladder work, with water-fed pole as backup for safety on taller exteriors. For taller buildings or buildings requiring rope access, we'll either subcontract a specialist or refer you to one we trust.

What's the minimum job size?

There's no hard minimum, but small storefront jobs are most cost-effective when bundled into a recurring schedule.

Can we pause service in the off-season?

Absolutely. Tourist-area businesses often run heavier schedules in summer and dial back in winter. We can adjust quarterly.
